Women in science initiatives

I'm a strong proponent of things being equal for everyone, but unless it's especially necessary, that opinion also extends to giving people special treatment to make up for previously unfair treatment. I know that women have historically been underrepresented in things such as science, and I definitely think everyone should be given an equal opportunity. However, when I hear time and time again about events empowering women in science or groups formed just for girls to learn and collaborate or colleges offering scholarships to women in science, and then I look around in a given biology class of 20 people and see 2 guys, I can't help but think that the dire need to rectify the past mistreatment of women in science has dissipated. Not being a girl, I can't provide any insight on the pressures girls face or what people may say to them in private, but although I've heard the occasional story of old views being perpetuated, I've never witnessed a girl ever being discouraged from pursuing a scientific career, and if I had, I could say with almost complete certainty that everyone in the room would consider such discouragement strongly distasteful. Maybe there's still a generation or two left of people who shy away from girls pursuing scientific careers, but it seems to me that the people in an age range most relevant to me (including older employers) have more or less left those thoughts behind. There will always be those few people who are just plain unreasonable, but they constitute a rather small minority. As a result of these thoughts and observations and the fact that, although I can't definitively say whether girls are brought down somewhat, I definitely don't feel built up, I hear about all of these initiatives for women in science and I wonder where my special treatment is. Why don't people go out of their way to say that I (a guy) can make it in science? Why don't I get to hang out with a bunch of guys and bounce ideas off of each other? Why am I forced to pay more money for my education just because of my gender? I'm inclined to believe that, at least for this generation, women in science initiatives have been so successful that, not only are they no longer necessary, but also, there should be something to help guys get some of the special treatment that has been given to girls for a while now. Not special beyond girls, just equal.
